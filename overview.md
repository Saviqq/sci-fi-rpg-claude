# GAME CONCEPT — Working Title TBD

## Core Premise

A 2D top-down pixel-art RPG (combat + dialogue, optionally stealth) set on a massive colony ship traveling between star systems.

After arriving at a supposedly habitable planet, the ship discovers a parasitic organism that infects all nervous systems, turning hosts into feral, unstable beings.

The leadership secretly decides to abandon colonization and return to Mars, believing the parasite could be exploited as a tool for human evolution and survival.

This truth is hidden from the population.

Over centuries, society collapses, fractures, and reforms into competing factions.

---

## Setting

**The Ship**
- A centuries-old colony vessel
- Originally pristine → now decayed, fragmented
- Divided into: controlled core zones, industrial sectors, abandoned "dead zones", parasite-infested regions

**Current State**
- The ship is en route back to Mars (unknown to most)
- Civilization has regressed into territorial factions
- Technology exists but is unevenly distributed, partially understood, often repurposed

---

## The Parasite

**Nature**
- Infects organisms with a nervous system
- Causes aggression, instability, loss of higher cognition
- Hosts become "Ferals"

**Key Property**
- Can be partially integrated with humans: grants strength, perception, resilience — but causes instability and loss of control

---

## Core Conflict

Three factions exist in a perfect stalemate. No one can fully win. Each holds a critical piece of power. The parasite is the center of everything.

---

## Factions

### Devoted (The System)
- **Role:** Authority / Order
- **Goal:** Regain full control, return to Mars, weaponize parasite
- **Strengths:** Control of life support, food, infrastructure; largest population; defensive power
- **Weaknesses:** No access to original parasite samples; internal paranoia (infiltration)
- **Methods:** Propaganda (population believes Opportunists control the ship); secret research into human-parasite integration to create controlled super-soldiers

### Opportunists (The Leverage)
- **Role:** Elite power brokers
- **Goal:** Maintain leverage and dominance
- **Strengths:** Control of original parasite samples and combat robots (strongest military force); untouchable due to deterrence
- **Weaknesses:** Very small population; cannot expand easily
- **Philosophy:** "Power is leverage. Survival is negotiation."

### Resistance (The Purists)
- **Role:** Saboteurs / Survivors
- **Goal:** Destroy parasite completely
- **Strengths:** Knowledge of abandoned zones; adaptability; strong ideological unity
- **Weaknesses:** Limited resources; internal moral conflict
- **Key Contradiction:** They hate the parasite — yet used it to create the player

---

## The Incident (Key Lore Event)

1. Devoted achieve breakthrough in parasite integration
2. Opportunist secret agent infiltrated in Devoted leadership
3. Agent attempts to steal research data
4. Data transferred via smugglers
5. Smugglers killed by Ferals in dead zones
6. Data + samples lost

**Aftermath:** Resistance scavengers discover fragmented data and damaged samples. They attempt to replicate the research to create immunity — and this leads to the creation of the player.

---

## Player Character

**Origin**
- A forgotten cryo human
- Modified using incomplete parasite research
- Created by the Resistance
- *You were not meant to survive.*

**Nature**
- Partial parasite integration (parasitic symbiosis)
- Unique state: not fully human, not fully infected

### Abilities

**Feral Sense**
The player's partial integration allows them to sense nearby Ferals — a passive warning that enemies are close before they become visible. This is the foundation of the player's unique survivability.

**Environmental Immunity** *(future)*
Parasitic symbiosis gradually grants immunity to environmental hazards: radiation, extreme cold, heat. Allows access to zones no other faction can enter.

### Progression — Dual Skill Tree

On level-up, the player spends points on one of two sides:

**Human side** — utility, social, exploration-focused abilities. Better outcomes in dialogue, interaction with factions, non-combat traversal.

**Parasite side** — combat-focused abilities. Increased strength, speed, and combat lethality. The more points invested here, the more "feral" the player becomes — affecting how NPCs and factions perceive and react to them.

There is no neutral path. Every choice pulls the player in one direction.

## Gameplay

**Style:** 2D true top-down (bird's eye view), real-time combat + stealth + exploration — reference points: Hotline Miami, Darkwood

**Perspective:** Camera looks straight down. Player sprite is designed from above. Player rotates freely to face the mouse cursor (360°). This enables a vision/FOV cone system that drives both atmosphere and stealth.

**Field of View:** Player has a visible light/shadow cone extending from their facing direction. Outside the cone, the environment is darkened or obscured — creating tension, limiting information, and rewarding tactical play. Ferals outside the FOV are hidden until the player turns toward them (except Feral Sense, which warns regardless of facing).

**Play Styles — both valid:**
- *Rambo:* Run and attack, use Feral Sense to avoid being flanked, brute-force through encounters
- *Tactical:* Use FOV cone to clear rooms methodically, avoid detection, conserve health

**Enemies**
- *Ferals:* Fast, erratic, semi-intelligent. Variants: stalkers, brutes, signalers
- *Human enemies — Devoted:* Organized soldiers, tech-based combat
- *Human enemies — Opportunists:* Elite units + combat robots

**Gameplay Pillars**
1. Exploration (decayed ship)
2. Combat (human + feral threats)
3. Stealth (FOV cone, sound, light — first-class, not an afterthought)
4. Narrative choice (faction alignment)
5. Progression (dual skill tree: Human vs Parasite)

- Control vs freedom
- Evolution vs preservation
